What are carbon footprints?

A carbon footprint is a way to measure greenhouse gas emissions. Greenhouse gases are gases such as carbon dioxide and methane that trap heat in the atmosphere and thereby contribute greatly to global warming. A carbon footprint is determined by the sum of these greenhouse gases that are released into the atmosphere either directly or indirectly by our actions (usually in tons per year). This can be applied to individuals, families, companies, events, products and even countries.

How to reduce carbon footprints?

Here are a few steps you must follow to reduce carbon footprints:

  • Consume local and seasonal products
  • Limit meat consumption, especially beef
  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle to waste less
  • Make sure to buy only what you need, to avoid waste
  • Switch to a low-carbon energy provider
  • Choose energy-efficient appliances
  • Travel less or travel wisely
